The Elements

John Dalton was an English chemist who came up with a theory called "The Atomic Theory of Elements". Dalton stated that all matter is made up of small, invisible atoms.


Atoms are the basic units of elements that make up air, water, soil, etc. An element is a substance that cannot be broken down into another basic unit. They make up everyone and everything in the universe!


Some examples of elements are oxygen, nitrogen, hydrogen, heilium, etc. Since elements are atoms, they make molecules! Molecules such as H²O (water) use the elements hydrogen (H) and oxygen (O). And since there is a "²" next to the H that means there are two hydrogen atoms.

After a few years of research of John Daltons theory, Russian chemist Dmitri Mendeleev started the periodic table of elements. Soon more elements were found and added to the table.


Some ways elements are used in real life are: in food, construction, medicine, chemistry, etc. Elements make up everything in life.


After many decades of research, there is a grand total of 118 elements (as of 2022). Many theories and questions about the elements have been solved, and any more ways to use elements have been discovered.
